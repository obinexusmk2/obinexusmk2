Constitutional Definition

What is OHA?

OHA represents the public protection layer of OBINexus governance. It is the constitutional guarantee that breathing rights are protected before any other consideration. OHA is the first gate: without it, IWU and IJI cannot function.

The OHA Guarantee:
Every human being — neurodivergent or neurotypical — enters the OBINexus constitutional framework under the protection of OHA. No system, institution, or authority may breach these protections. Protection precedes production.

Core Principles

The OHA Covenant

I

Breathing Rights are Non-Negotiable

The right to breathe — physically, cognitively, sensorially — cannot be traded, suspended, or conditioned. It precedes all legal frameworks. OHA encodes this as the zeroth law of OBINexus governance.

II

Public Infrastructure Serves the Neurodivergent First

When infrastructure is designed for the most excluded, it works for everyone. OHA mandates that all OBINexus public systems are built neurodivergent-first — from sensory accessibility to cognitive load management.

III

Protection Precedes Production

No economic, political, or operational priority overrides the OHA guarantee. Before any system produces output, it must first establish that the people within it are constitutionally protected.

Legal Infrastructure

Human Rights Defense Framework

OHA maps to existing UK, UN, and Biafran legal frameworks. Constitutional mathematics establishes value for each violation.

Housing Act 1996

Section 184 — Independent Housing Rights

Right to independent assessment of housing need. Section 184 Appeals preserve the neurodivergent person's right to adequate, accessible shelter.

Human Rights Act 1998

Article 8 — Right to Private and Family Life

No public authority shall interfere with the exercise of this right except as permitted by law. Includes the right to cognitive and sensory autonomy.

Mental Health Act 1983

Section 117 — After-Care Entitlements

Indefinite after-care covering housing support at no cost. Section 115(1)-(2) entry rights for approved mental health professionals.

Children Act 1989

Section 21 — Duty to Accommodate

Local authority duty to provide accommodation to persons in need. Care leavers aged 18–25 retain protections under Health and Social Care Act 2014.
